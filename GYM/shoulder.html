Shoulder Workouts

About

Hello! welcome Shoulder Workouts. Doing shoulder exercises strengthens the shoulders and relieves pain. Doing shoulder exercises also increases flexibility. This makes the body strong and flexible. Doing shoulder exercises also improves the shape of the body.

Types

Arnold Press

Stand holding dumbbells in each hand at shoulder height, palms facing your chest, to start. Press the dumbbells upward while simultaneously twisting your wrists so you finish with your palms facing away from you as you lock out your elbows. Reverse the motion as you lower. That's 1 rep. Note: You can do this sitting or standing.

90-90 External Rotation

Stand tall, holding a 5- to 10- pound dumbbell in each hand, to start. Raise arms so dumbbells are level with head and elbows are at 90 degrees. Rotating only your shoulders, keeping elbows and wrists in same positions, lower weights forward so forearms are parallel to ground and palms face up. Reverse the movement to return to start. That's 1 rep.

Standing Dumbbell Flye

Hold a dumbbell in each hand by your sides, palms facing down, to start. Without shrugging, use your upper body to swing the weights up a few inches. Your arms and torso will form an upside down V-shape. Think of it as a lateral raise with momentum but without full range of motion. That's 1 rep.

Benifits

Arnold Press

Maximize Your Mass Gain. Increase Range Of Motion. Overall Wellness. Improve Your Posture.

90-90 External Rotation

improves hip mobility, which can help with pain in the back, knees, and feet.

Standing Dumbbell Flye
