Types

Pressure Fighter

A pressure fighter in boxing is a boxer who uses a relentless, high-energy style to wear down their opponent. Pressure fighters fight aggressively in close quarters, bombarding their opponent with heavy attacks.

Out-Boxer

The out-boxer. The out-boxer (outside fighter, boxer) seeks to stay well outside of their opponent's punching range when disengaged and land long-range punches..

Counter Puncher

A counter puncher in boxing is a fighter who uses a combination of offense and defense to gain an advantage over their opponent.

Tips

Pressure Fighter

To be an effective pressure fighter in boxing, focus on constantly moving forward, utilizing a strong jab to control distance, throwing punches in combinations while advancing, targeting the body to disrupt defense, and maintaining good head movement to minimize counter punches while always looking to land powerful shots; essentially, never let your opponent dictate the pace by aggressively pushing them back throughout the fight.
